Common Operational Failures

If you recognise these,
you're leaking money.

Most business problems aren't strategy problems — they're operational problems. Here's what we see most often, and what it costs.

🕳️

Revenue Leakage

Deals fall through cracks. Follow-ups don't happen. Renewals get missed. Invoices sit unpaid. None of it shows up cleanly — but all of it costs money.

  • Lost leads between handoffs
  • Missed renewal and upsell windows
  • Unbilled work and delayed invoicing
  • Poor lead-to-close tracking
🔀

Broken Handoffs

Information dies between departments. Sales doesn't tell ops. Ops doesn't tell delivery. Delivery doesn't tell finance. Every gap is a problem waiting to happen.

  • Sales-to-delivery communication gaps
  • Manual data re-entry between systems
  • Context loss across team members
  • Accountability black holes
📋

Manual Everything

Your best people spend hours on tasks that shouldn't require human intelligence. Copying and pasting. Chasing updates. Building the same spreadsheet every week.

  • Manual reporting and dashboards
  • Human-driven data entry and transfer
  • Repetitive email and notification tasks
  • Spreadsheet dependency at scale
🌑

Zero Visibility

No one knows what's actually happening in the business until something goes wrong. By then, the cost is already incurred. Real-time data is an afterthought.

  • No real-time pipeline or delivery view
  • Lagging financial and operational data
  • KPIs tracked manually in spreadsheets
  • Leaders making decisions on gut, not data
📈

Scaling Failure

Growth exposes every gap. The systems that worked at £500k don't work at £2M. More clients means more chaos, more staff means more coordination cost.

  • Processes that break under volume
  • Founder still doing everything important
  • Hiring to solve process problems
  • Costs growing faster than revenue
🔧

Tech Stack Chaos

14 tools. None talking to each other. Half the team using workarounds. Duplicate data. Subscriptions no one uses. And no one knows where the truth lives.

  • Siloed systems with no integration
  • Multiple sources of truth
  • Shadow tools and workarounds
  • Redundant subscriptions and costs
